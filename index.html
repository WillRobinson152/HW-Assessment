Home Buyers Look to Midwest in Week’s Immediate Sales

by Will Robinson

June 21, 2023

The Midwest housing market was a favorite among buyers in the week ended June 2, according to Altos Research data on immediate sales.

Amongst sellers that accepted offers before their homes spent time on the active market, Illinois topped the charts with 1,549 immediate sales. It netted about 200 more sales than the next highest on the list, Tennessee. Fellow Midwest state Missouri also recorded more than 1,000 sales, and Ohio finished just below 1,000.

The region also punched above its weight with a higher number of sales per thousand residents than more populous states like California and Texas, according to 2022 population estimates by the U.S. Census Bureau.

Missouri tallied 0.18 immediate house sales per thousand residents, behind only Connecticut at 0.2 and Tennessee at 0.19. Illinois also saw a strong 0.12 immediate house sales per thousand residents.

Peoria helped Illinois’s strong week with 233 immediate sales, more than double the next highest city, Jacksonville, Florida. Other Midwestern cities in the top 10 of immediate home sales include Columbus, Dayton and Wichita.

The Midwest’s appeal may lie in its affordability. There are no Midwestern cities on the lists of the 10 most expensive cities ranked by median sales price or by median sales prices versus per capita personal income, as estimated by the U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis.

Midwestern states also contrast favorably against coastal states in terms of the states’ median sales price and their median sales price versus median household income. If affordability is the impetus behind the Midwest’s sales, the present environment of high interest rates bodes well for the region’s sales volume going forward.

Nationally, the week’s 20,554 immediate sales of single-family homes, condominiums and townhomes remains set in the long-running context of tight supply.

U.S. single-family inventory stood at 436,284 homes as of June 2, according to Altos Research. That is the highest level since Feb. 17 and is about 68,000 more houses than at the same point last year.

However, it is a far cry from pre-pandemic levels. The national inventory totaled more than 967,000 homes as recently as June 28, 2019.

Outside of the Midwest, Tennessee and North Carolina competed strongly in immediate sales on the week.

Tennessee’s 1,344 immediate sales were the second most in the country, and Knoxville’s 65 immediate sales made it the 4th most active city. North Carolina’s 1,142 immediate sales came in 4th in the nation and was more than Texas’ 957 or California’s 778.
